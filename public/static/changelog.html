  • Release 2.0.0 • Build 1233 • October 20, 2020

    New Things!

    • Remchi is now open source! Named OpenVUE, you can find the full source code on GitHub.
    • Complete refactoring of the Remchi core:
      • This is the portion Remchi uses to communicate with Synergy, and it has been slowly accumulating bugs ever since its rocky start back in February 2019.
      • By redoing it from scratch using modern APIs, it now fully supports concurrent enrollment schools, has better caching and more detailed errors, and is significantly less prone to bugs.
    • Redesigned dashboard:
      • See the weather, date, and time (on desktop) at a glance
      • Concurrently enrolled with another school? See all of your classes at once!
      • Unobtrusive banner messages (in contrast to the obnoxious popups in the past)
    • Significantly better accessibility:
      • Almost all of Remchi can now be navigated with just a keyboard!
      • Better "Reduced Motion" setting removes even more optional animations
    • Data Exporting:
      • You can now download your grades in a variety of formats!
      • Currently XML, JSON, and XLSX are supported

    Improvements!

    • Performance improvements:
      • Code optimization and dead code cleanup means pages should load even faster than before
      • The legacy, buggy course list loading system has been thrown out and replaced with a proper VueJS implementation
    • Greatly improved "Welcome" carousel for the new user onboarding experience
    • Lots and lots and lots of bug fixes:
      • Far too many to list here, but they can be found in the full changelog!

    What's next?

    • Potential notifier overhaul:
      • Reintroduce text notifications to the general public if possible?
      • Plans to greatly improve stability and customization options
    • You tell me! We're always looking for suggestions on how Remchi can be further improved.

  • Release 1.0.0 • Build 1,000 • January 6, 2020

    What's New

    Release 1.0.0 • Build 1000 • January 1, 2020
    Missed a release? See Previous and/or Full Changelog

    And a quick PSA...

    • Heads Up! Due to the size of this release, some features have been partially rolling out to users since December. You may have already seen some of these new features!
    • Thanks again to everyone who took the second survey! Got lots of valuable feedback; check the Roadmap tab (under "More Actions") to see the data.

    Fancy New Stuff

    • GPA and Course History! Very very heavily requested, but was rather difficult from a technological standpoint. But hey, it works now!
    • Full What-If Support! Not only can you edit any existing assignment, you can also add new ones now.
    • New Pages: Account and Full Changelog (/account and /changelog respectively).
      • Use the "Account" page to see where you've logged in, how often you check gradebook, and (as a bonus) how that compares to everyone else! Link in the "More Actions" dropdown.
      • Changelog is pretty self-explanatory, link at the top of this dialog.
      • Not technically new, but the landing page has been completely redone. Check it out sometime!
    • Attendance Improvements: The attendance page now features a calendar, with a dot on any day you have attendance marks.
    • A Welcome Screen! New users can choose to get a quick crash course now. Want to see it for yourself? Go to Settings -> About -> Show Welcome Screen.
    • Any district support which won't affect current users, but Remchi should now work with any district using Synergy!

    Other Changes

    • A heaping pile of bugfixes and optimizations. Feeling nerdy? A technical description of every change is listed under the new full changelog. Here's some of the more notable ones:
      • Replaced the up arrow in a course with a left "return" arrow, since most people didn't use the up arrow.
      • Pending items now a) show their total point value and b) can be directly used in Grade Calculator
      • Remchi looks a lot better on mobile in general, especially on really small screens (where previously text was constantly cut off)
      • Behind the scenes: Fixed a months-long issue with weird dropdowns on iOS, users being asked to sign in twice, gradebook not updating, and many other obscure (but critical) bugs.

    What Else?

    • Able/want to help? Share the app with your friends especially if you're an underclassmen. Having interested users means I'm not throwing money at hosting/domains for nothing :D
      If you're curious, currently (as of this release) 12.4% of Fossil students have used Remchi in December, almost double compared to May last school year!
  • Open Beta 5.0.0 • Build 921 • October 20, 2019

    Fancy New Stuff

    • Thanks to everyone who took the survey! The two most requested features were trends and editing, both of which are now features along with some other things.
    • Trends! See how your grade changed over time with the new Trends tool! Click/tap on the number to show your grade at the time.
    • Editing! See how changing your grade on a particular assignment (or two) affects your overall grade. You can even edit pending items!
    • Pending indicators which show ungraded items as "pending" rather than "new"- requested quite a bit in the past. These will switch to "new" once they're graded.
    • Adaptive color scheme matching your system's, so Remchi will be more consistent the rest of your platform.
    • The URL is respected now, so if you bookmark the link to a specific course, it'll actually work.

    Other Changes

    • More customization, in fact, so much that there's now two settings tabs! All features shown above can be disabled through settings. Along with new customization tools.
    • More bug fixes, let me know if you encounter any!
    • Bunch of small tweaks, such as courses now show their room/location.

    What Else?

    • Able/want to help? Share the app with your friends especially if you're not a senior - I'm not looking for monetary gains through Remchi, but having interested users means I'm not throwing money at hosting/domains for nothing :D
      If you're curious, currently (as of this release) 2.6% of Fossil students have used Remchi in the last two weeks, about half the size of May last school year.
    • Speaking of apps, you know there's an installable mobile app for Remchi, right?
    • This will likely be the last major open beta release before either moving into the prerelease / release phase! (There's a known issue with dropdowns behaving weirdly on iOS 13 that I'd like to patch before full release)

  • Open Beta 4.0.0 • Build 818 • August 18, 2019

    Fancy New Stuff

    • The sidebar has gotten a lot fuller! Three new modules (detailed below) have been implemented, doubling what we previously had.
    • Bell Schedules! Currently for Fossil only, but great for getting yourself oriented with the new schedule. Even tells you how long you have left in a class!
    • Attendance Records and Messages - You won't need the other StudentVUE app to check these anymore!
    • Custom backgrounds so you can finally put your face on top of your grades. Or not. Doesn't matter, you have options now!
    • Browser buttons work now so pressing back will actually do what you'd expect it to do. You can't bookmark a specific page quite yet - we're working on that!
    • More detailed "More Info" modals now showing things like Date Due, why the assignment was highlighted (if it was), and more.
    • Extra credit assignments are now more apparent than ever, and will simply say "EC".
    • Automatic app updates so any installed apps will always be put on the latest version of Remchi... and it only updates the components needed so it'll usually take <10 mb.
    • Offline app use which was half-implemented previously but only somewhat worked. You can now take your phone offline and still access your gradebook and bell schedule after loading it once!
    • This modal is new too if you didn't notice, so you don't have to guess what changed anymore. It's also accessible under the "More Actions" button.

    Some Other Tidbits

    • Many many bugs were fixed between the previous major release and now, but we're certain there's more. Please let us know if you encounter any!
    • Notifier is disabled unless you were with us in the 2018-19 year. You can still request access manually, but the reason for this is in the next point.
    • Significant changes in data handling (and our legal pages) because while most students could care less about what info they give/store, district IT personnel have expressed potential concerns, and we're taking proactive measures to both collect as little data as possible and be extremely open in what we do collect. Specifics are detailed in our Privacy Policy.
    • We're almost out of Open Beta! The big features envisioned last year are more or less complete now. After testing the big features a bit and smoothing out the smaller issues, we'll officially go into Build 1000!
